About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Computer and Information Science at the University of Michigan-Dearborn, where I work in the Database Research Lab under the supervision of Professor Niccolò Meneghetti. My background includes software engineering and research experience across machine learning, systems, software optimization, and parallel computing. I am always eager to learn and grow.

Download CV






Research areas: My research integrates Bayesian machine learning into database cores to natively handle uncertain and probabilistic data. I enable databases to perform statistical reasoning directly, managing uncertainty and delivering reliable insights from complex, incomplete data. To achieve this, I work at the intersection of data management, machine learning, and probabilistic programming. I develop query execution engines that bridge relational queries and Bayesian inference while maintaining high performance.

Selected Publications

  1. StarfishDB: A Query Execution Engine for Relational Probabilistic Programming
    Ouael Ben Amara*, Sami Hadouaj*, and Niccolò Meneghetti
    Proc. ACM Manag. Data, May 2024
    Abs DOI

    We introduce StarfishDB, a query execution engine optimized for relational probabilistic programming. Our engine adopts the model of Gamma Probabilistic Databases, representing probabilistic programs as a collection of relational constraints, imposed against a generative stochastic process. We extend the model with the support for recursion, factorization and the ability to leverage just-in-time compilation techniques to speed up inference. We test our engine against a state-of-the-art sampler for Latent Dirichlet Allocation.
